Background

Benford's law states that in many naturally occurring collections of numbers, the leading digit is observed to be '1' about 30% of the time, with the succeeding digits following a decreasing power law trend.

This observation can be used to detect dataset manipulation or falsification, in applications such as accounting, natural sciences, or experimental data. There are examples where this trend does not hold true - particularly where all digits have an equal likelihood of being the leading digit. For example, there would not be many NBA players shorter than 5 feet - generally they would range from 5 - 7 feet. Also, random number generators would be expected to have an even digit distribution - so Benford's law would not apply. Small sample sizes and datasets that do no span several orders of magnitude may also deviate from Benford's law such as a person's weight or IQ score.

Instructions

You may submit your own dataset to test against Benford's Law. This web application will plot your data against the expected Benford distribution, and also perform a Chi-Squared Test to see if your data conforms to Benford's Law.
